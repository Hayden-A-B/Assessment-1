Project Ideas

Overview

Digital Smart Calendar with possibilities for personal, business or public utilization.

You could have multiple digital calendars which can all be updated simultaneously from a single control point such as a computer running a synced application or simply leave it without any syncing and input calendar events from the device directly using either touchscreen input or speech to text.

A major use would be for vision impaired people as the device would have full voice control and can read aloud weekly events, tell you what day or time it is and similar features when asked via verbal commands.

Motivation

The Digital Smart Calendar would be a way of sharing events with household members or for business settings. In a home setting a Smart Calendar would allow people to view another person’s schedule Being like a standard tablet but having a narrowly defined use case will allow unnecessary functions to be left out so that the cost and complexity of design can be reduced.

Description

It would be asleep while not in use and may be motion sensor activated when someone walks a metre in front of it or turned on when someone turns on their smart lights. Options would allow the calendar to be a standalone product or able to sync with another calendar service or multiple services to serve as a central calendar for a collective of people.

Features

  • Standby would display a monthly overview and notes for daily events and icons indicating there are hidden events of lesser priority.
  • Clock and timers.
  • Expandable view of daily events when a day or timeframe is are selected
  • Send tasks from a public calendar to a private calendar without exposing your personal information.
  • View daily agenda, and colour schemes for different people and opacity determines priority
  • Smart home control panel for lights or other connected devices.
  • Modes for read only so that the calendar can be put on display in a public area.
  • Motion sensor activated, triggered when a room is ‘active’ possibly determined by the lights being turned on.
  • Standalone or syncing with major calendar apps and todo lists (e.g., Google, Apple, and Microsoft)
  • To-do/shopping list.
  • Notes with optional handwriting input, recorded notes.
  • Syncs via Wi-Fi.
  • Speakers and read aloud for vision impaired, with speech to text functions.
  • Automatically populate calendar with major localised events (e.g., Public Holiday)
